MIN_UPSET_RATE = 0.05  # At least 5% of simulations have 10+ seed in Sweet 16

**Function `check_upset_rate(mc_result, seedings, team_seed_map)`:**
- Takes the Monte Carlo result dict from simulate_bracket(mode='monte_carlo').
- For each team, check if they appear in any Sweet 16 slot's advancement probability.
- Compute the upset rate: fraction of teams with seed >= 10 that have non-zero Sweet 16 advancement probability. Actually, the success criterion is about SIMULATIONS, not teams. We need to check: "at least 5% of 10,000 simulations show a 10-or-higher seed reaching the Sweet 16."
- Approach: Use the advancement_probs to compute this. For each team with seed >= 10, get their Sweet 16 probability. Use inclusion-exclusion or simpler: since these are across different regions, the probability that NO 10+ seed reaches Sweet 16 is the product of (1 - P(team reaches Sweet 16)) across all 10+ seeds. Then P(at least one) = 1 - product.
- Actually, the most accurate way: re-inspect the occupants arrays from Monte Carlo. But those are not stored in the result dict. Instead, use advancement_probs as a proxy. For each run, the probability that at least one 10+ seed is in Sweet 16 can be computed from the per-team advancement probs.
- **Simpler approach**: Sum the Sweet 16 probabilities of all 10+ seeds. If the sum >= MIN_UPSET_RATE, the check passes (this is conservative -- the sum overestimates the probability of at least one because events overlap, but for a sanity check it is directionally correct).
- **Best approach**: Use the complement formula. P(no 10+ upset) = product of (1 - p_sweet16) for all 10+ seeds. P(at least one) = 1 - product.
- Compute and return the upset rate as a float.
- If below MIN_UPSET_RATE, issue a `warnings.warn()` (do NOT raise an exception):
  ```python
  warnings.warn(
      f"Monte Carlo upset rate is {upset_rate:.1%} "
      f"(expected >= {MIN_UPSET_RATE:.0%}). "
      "Model may be overconfident -- check ClippedCalibrator application.",
      stacklevel=2,
  )
  ```
- Return the upset rate.
